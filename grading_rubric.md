The assessment for this course has two components: 1) Group discussions from week 4 onwards (20%) 2) Team Project (80%)

For the project, there will be two presentations (20%) and a final report (60%). You should develop a project where you collect and analyze social media data to answer a social research question. You should clearly articulate the question and use computational approaches for both data collection and data analysis. 

## Project Report

You may get up to 9 points in bonus for the project report, however, the report score is capped at 60. Bonus points achieved in the report cannot be used to make up for lower scores in class discussion or the presentations.

- **Abstract**: 250-word summary of your project including a formulation of its research questions and an overview of methods and results [5]

- **Motivation**: What question(s) do you seek to answer and why? Elaborate on how this research matters in terms of science, technology and/or society [5]

- **Research background**: What previous work is related to this project and what can we learn from it? Do not just cite papers but highlight what's relevant to your work. Reflect about the cited contributions on your text [5]

- **Data**: Describe your data sources including descriptive statistics and plots. Carefully document all references to data sources. [8]

- **Data Quality**: Describe how you checked the quality of your dataset. [2]

- **Methods**: Explain how you filtered data, normalized values, computed additional variables, etc. Detail the statistical analyses and other methods you will apply to assess your research question(s). Carefully document all references to methods and packages used. [8]

- **Validation**: Describe how you validate your methods. [2]

- **Results**: Expose the results of your analysis including tables and figures that communicate and illustrate those results [15]

- **Conclusion and Discussion**: Evaluate answers to the question and their reliability. Identify limitations and alternative explanations for your results. [5]

- **Code and Dataset**: Your codebase and dataset (or a sample of it if it's too large) should be available in a Github repository with sufficient documentation to ensure reproducibility. [5]

Note: Remember to include author contributions in the final report (After the conclusion).

### Bonus points (marked in the project report)
- +5 if you collect your own dataset rather than reusing an existing dataset
- +2 for a multiplatform study
- +2 for a multilingual study, i.e., you study data spanning multiple languages

## Project Presentations 

All members of a group do not have to present but all members should be prepared to answer questions. If you cannot attend the project presentation, please email the instructors beforehand. Please stick to the time. If your presentation is over time, we will deduct half a point for every minute you go over.

### Project Pitches [5 minutes for each team, 5 points]

- Research question and motivation [4]: include one slide that briefly describes the following:
	- construct
	- platform(s)
	- data collection strategy
	- data analysis strategy
- project timeline [1]

### Midterm Presentation [15 minutes for each team, 15 points]

- Brief research question and motivation [1]
- Data and methods [5]
- Preliminary results [3]
- Planned next steps [1]
